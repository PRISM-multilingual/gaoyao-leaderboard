About

皋陶 — named for the legendary judge of ancient China, evaluating language models across 26 languages and 51 cultures with the fairness the name implies.

皋 陶
Gāo Yáo · ~2200 BCE

About the name

皋陶 (Gāo Yáo) is the legendary minister of justice under Emperor Shun (~2200 BCE), revered in Chinese tradition as the founder of impartial law. We borrowed the name in the spirit that evaluating multilingual models requires the same impartiality across languages and cultures that the ancient sage was said to embody.

From the Book of Documents: Emperor Shun appointed Gāo Yáo as the Grand Justice (士), tasked with administering justice across all peoples — barbarians and Chinese alike. He is venerated as the founder of impartial Chinese law.

The Framework

GaoYao organises evaluation along three orthogonal cultural layers and ten cognitive sub-layers (mirroring paper Fig. 5):

  • General Multilingual — Math · Reasoning · Knowledge · Instruction Following · Dialogue · Reading · Translation
  • Cross-cultural — SAGE · SuperBLEnD
  • Monocultural — CultureScope

Coverage: 182.3k samples · 26 languages · 51 nations / areas.

Score Source

All scores on this leaderboard are computed from the benchmark's evaluation results — per-language raw outputs that we aggregate at runtime with score₀₋₁₀₀ = max(0, raw × 100), mean over languages within a sub-task. The same source feeds the overall table, by-language table, dataset×language heatmaps, F3 boxplot, and F4 thinking-gain panel.

  • Objective MCQ / math / reading: accuracy × 100
  • Translation (Flores-101): COMET-derived score × 100, clipped at 0
  • Subjective (S-AlpacaEval / S-MT-Bench): LLM-as-judge win-rate × 100

Maintained by PRISM

PRISM is a research team focused on multilingual and multicultural large language models. The name captures the team's mission — a prism splits one beam of light into the full visible spectrum; we aim for a single LLM to express the full spectrum of human languages and cultures, faithfully and fairly.

Beyond GaoYao, PRISM ships multilingual benchmarks, evaluation tooling, and curated multilingual data pipelines.
